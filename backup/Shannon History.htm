Intel Shannon Site History

In June 1999, Basis Communications Corporation undertook an extensive tour of locations in Europe and Ireland to select a suitable European location. This was completed in August when Basis Communications Europe Limited opened its EMEA headquarters at the e-Com Centre in Ennis, Co. Clare. This office provided business, Sales and Technical Support to Basis Communications customers in Europe/Middle East and Africa.

In January of 2000 this role was expanded, an R&D group was recruited and Basis moved to the Shannon Free Zone. In the following months, the R&D team was rapidly expanded and charged with the development of software for the “Thunder” Network Processor, and later, the “Monsoon” network processor. These Network Processors (NP’s) were among the first of their kind to be introduced to the world-wide communications market and represented the most advanced and technically innovative devices then available. The software, developed at Shannon, included both low-level firmware and some applications oriented towards certain key vertical markets, including low-end residential gateway network devices, communications set-top boxes and early ADSL controllers.

In May 2000 Intel Corporation acquired Basis Communications and Shannon continued to provide software for the Monsoon (now renamed the IXP22X) Network Processor. Plans were immediately put in place to increase the size of the R&D team and a number of new projects were investigated

During 2000, Intel Communications Europe (as the Shannon site was now renamed) began research on a new Network Processor. This was a second generation device and many times more powerful than the previous Monsoon Processor. The software for the device was jointly developed between Intel Communications Europe and a sister research and development facility (also part of the Basis acquisition), in Westford, Massachusetts. The Silicon design was carried out in Shannon. This exciting collaboration resulted in the on-time launch of the IXP/425 Network Processor which was released worldwide in Q2, 2002 and which is manufactured by the Intel Ireland FAB in Leixlip, Co. Kildare.

In 2006, Intel Communications Europe Ltd was renamed Intel Shannon Ltd. The change has been made to more accurately portray the diversity of activity on campus which is no longer solely communications centric. We are striving to play our part in making IA the architecture of choice in the communications, embedded and storage market segments.
